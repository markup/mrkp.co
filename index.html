This is how it rolls.

/markup is essentially a one-man operation run by Alfonso Gómez-Arzola (that would be the handsome fellow to the right). Alfonso designs and develops the front end of his client’s projects, while collaborating with trusted back-end developers on select projects which require it. On occassion, he’ll collaborate with other designers who rely on his code-wrangling skills to bring their designs to fruition. This small yet collaborative approach keeps /markup’s operation lean whilst retaining the capacity to handle complex projects.

Alfonso has a background in print design. A self-taught designer, his unorthodox career path has led him to work with a variety of clients under several design firms. Most notably, he has worked with the United States Olympic Committee, Banco Popular de Puerto Rico, Museum of Contemporary Art of Puerto Rico, NJCAA, and Camera Bits; in collaboration with designers such as Alberto Rigau (estudio interlínea) and Mun Choi (Axio Design), as well as creative firm Momenta Creative, all of whom he still collaborates with regularly.

On top of all this, Alfonso seems to have no qualms about writing of himself in the third person, although speaking of himself in this manner is yet to be observed. For more information, please use the contact form below to get in touch.
